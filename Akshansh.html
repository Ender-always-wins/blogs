You're probably wondering who is this guy and why do we wanna read anything about him. To be honest... I don't even know, what my plan is for this section of the website, I might not have really thought it through completely before deciding to put up this section honestly lol.

Well, today I guess a bit of an introduction is the best way to start this. So, lets just start by why do I even have this website up or why do I even want to be an engineer, or why am I interested in cybersecurity at all. It's kind of a funny story actually, unlike many others, the first calling of my life had never been anything engineering related, at all. My initial career choice, decided around 2020, was to be an Actuary. I was scouring the web for what I should do in order to be a good actuary and I found out knowing python or R might help.

Right around that time, this platform called Whitehat Junior was really prominent, I was hearing about python there as well. So to the 10-11 year old me, it sounded like the best way forward, and due to the pandemic I had lots of free time either way. So I just downloaded the language, learnt how to set up vs code, and began the journey. The youtube channels, Freecodecamp.org, Programming With Mosh and Bro Code were an integral part of the journey.

Then slowly but surely I learnt the language, and later I found out about competitive programming and data structures and algorithms. To be honest, I was not really all that good back then, at that time I was did not really know what I was doing all that for. I didn't understand how that would help me with any project or anything, but it was just really fun so I continued.

Then near the end of 8th grade, I found out more about the existence of engineers, and engineering colleges. Naturally, the name IIT came up, as does to every Indian kid aspiring to be an engineer. I found the competitive programming problems I had looked at earlier would directly help me with being an engineer, and it was one of the more fun things I knew at the time. So, thats how I found out I might want to be an engineer, but then I made a not so ideal decision to cut programming out of my life, entirely in order to focus on math olympiads, which seemed quiet fascinating to the 13 or 14 year old me.

Well, due to not having enough structure because my main resource was youtube, I might not have succeeded in the olympiad world, but it definitely prepared me for what was to come ahead. A huge part of the journey was facilitated because I had tasted what new and tough problems actually looked like, which really helped build problem solving skills, and developed a structured way to think.

After the last olympiad season (which was when I in grade 10th) that I participated in, I enrolled in a JEE coaching, but right before the classes were to begin I had a month or so of free time. During that time, I found out about cybersecurity, and learnt about TryHackMe. That month, I learnt about VMs, some linux fundamentals, some OSINT tricks, and explored a few rooms to learn about tools like nmap, dirbuster, wireshark, etc., that was one of the most fun activities I had partaken in. To be honest, I wasn't really interested in physics or chemistry, but IITs were the clearest and most importantly the safest path, that I could find for the careers that seemed appealing to me. So I had to take PCM stream, just so I could sit for JEE and get into an IIT, and as we all know, JEE needs full focus and attention throughout your prep, so I could not really continue with TryHackMe further while wrapped in the all-encompassing abyss of JEE.

Now that the exam is over, I plan to brush up on my programming skills, and cybersecurity fundamentals once again so I can continue to move towards my main goals. If you have any tips or advice for me, feel free to contact me through any of the socials.
